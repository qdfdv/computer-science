Internet Technologies

The internet connects millions of devices worldwide. It allows people to communicate, share information, and access websites, videos, and applications instantly. Learning how it works helps us understand DNS, broadband, and how data travels across networks.

Fun Fact: The first website went online in 1991!

Cloud Computing

Cloud computing allows storing and accessing data on remote servers instead of local devices. This technology makes collaboration easy, enables access from any device, and powers services like Google Drive, Dropbox, and streaming platforms.

Fun Fact: Cloud computing powers Gmail, Netflix, and Spotify!

Data Transmission

Data transmission is the movement of information between devices. It can happen through wired cables, fiber optics, or wireless signals. Reliable transmission is critical to ensure that data arrives safely and quickly, without corruption.
